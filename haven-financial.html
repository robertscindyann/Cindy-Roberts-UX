Designing Trust in Digital Banking

UX Case Study — Haven Financial Onboarding Flow

  • Role: UX Designer
  • Timeline: 1 week
  • Tools: Figma
  • Deliverables: User flow, wireframes

This case study showcases the design of a mobile banking app onboarding experience for Haven Financial — a conceptual brand emphasizing trust, simplicity, and clarity. The goal was to create an intuitive, step-by-step flow that helps users open and fund a new checking account while feeling secure and informed.

The Problem

Many users abandon banking apps due to confusing verification steps and unclear progress. The challenge was to design a streamlined, confidence-building flow that made setup easy, secure, and transparent. This assumption was reinforced by secondary research and app review analysis, which revealed frustration around unclear guidance and lack of trust in digital banking.

  • Users often feel anxious about sharing sensitive information online.
  • Complex forms and unclear instructions lead to frustration and drop-off.
  • Trust is crucial in financial services, especially for first-time users.

Research Validation & Design Goals

As a conceptual UX project, this work was informed by secondary research and competitive analysis of leading personal finance apps such as Mint, YNAB, and Empower. By analyzing app store reviews, UX articles, and discussions in personal finance communities, I identified recurring patterns around user trust, complexity, and motivation.

Key Insights

  • Users feel anxious sharing financial data when the app lacks transparent communication or visual reassurance.
  • Excessive form fields and unclear verification steps increase friction and abandonment rates.
  • Visual simplicity and clear progress indicators boost perceived trust and confidence.

This analysis revealed that apps like YNAB achieved higher trust through transparency and calm design, while Mint’s longer onboarding and technical tone created friction. These findings guided Haven’s focus on simplicity, reassurance, and visual calm.

Design Goals

  • Trust-building: Use color, tone, and microcopy to reassure users through secure steps.
  • Clarity: Make each step purpose-driven and visually distinct.
  • Progress visibility: Help users see where they are in the process.
  • Efficiency: Minimize input fields and leverage smart defaults.
  • Visual calm: Apply Haven’s navy blue and teal palette to evoke stability and ease.

User Flow

The onboarding process was structured as a guided flow: Welcome → Open an Account → Choose Account Type → Verify Identity → Fund Account → Success.

Design System Highlights

  • Colors: Navy (#1B2B4D) and Teal (#38B6AB) for brand trust and calm.
  • Typography: Inter for modern clarity and hierarchy.
  • Scale: Text sizes based on the Golden Ratio for visual balance.
  • Buttons: Rounded edges and soft gradients for accessibility and friendliness.

Screen Designs

Each screen was designed to guide the user smoothly through the onboarding process while reinforcing trust at every touchpoint.

Key UX Decisions

Each UX decision focused on making the onboarding process feel guided, secure, and rewarding — balancing trust with a frictionless experience.

  • Guided Simplicity: Each screen focused on one clear action at a time, preventing cognitive overload and allowing users to move forward confidently.
  • Trust Through Visual Language: The navy and teal palette provided calm and credibility, while white space and soft gradients created a professional yet welcoming interface.
  • Progress & Reassurance: A three-step progress indicator — “Step 1 of 3: Personal Info,” “Step 2 of 3: Verify,” and “Step 3 of 3: Funding” — reinforced momentum and transparency.
  • Microcopy & Tone: Plain, human language such as “We’re verifying your information securely” helped users feel safe without overwhelming them with compliance terms.
  • Inline Feedback: Real-time form validation and green success states provided immediate reassurance that details were entered correctly.
  • Completion Reinforcement: The success screen featured a checkmark icon, the heading “Your checking account has been funded!”, body text “You can now start using your new Haven Checking account.”, and a single Done button — giving users closure and a sense of achievement.

Impact

  • Increased confidence through consistent microcopy and visual cues.
  • Reduced form friction by simplifying input fields and progress visibility.
  • Established scalability for future product extensions like loans or savings.

Research Limitations & Next Steps

Because Haven Financial was a conceptual project, I used assumptive and secondary research rather than direct user interviews. Future iterations would include usability testing and moderated interviews to validate clarity, trust signals, and task completion rates.

Reflection & Next Steps

This project helped me strengthen my ability to design for trust — balancing financial compliance with emotional reassurance.

  • Next Iteration: Expand usability testing to validate clarity and error handling.
  • Future Direction: Extend Haven into a personalized dashboard experience integrating savings and credit tracking.

Refreshing a High School Team Identity

Brand Case Study — Wildcats Dance Team Logo System

  • Role: Brand/Visual Designer
  • Timeline: 5 weeks
  • Stakeholders: Coach & School Principal
  • Tools: Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop
  • Deliverables: Primary/alt logos, usage guidelines, apparel mockups

The Wildcats Dance Team wanted a refreshed identity that felt modern and unified across uniforms and school events. Initial ideas included a new name (“Engage”) and a crossword-style mark. My goal was to align vision, tradition, and production constraints into a simple, scalable system that still felt proudly “Wildcats.”

The Problem

  • Coach wanted a crossword-inspired design and considered renaming the team to “Engage.”
  • “Engage” carried meaning but risked losing recognition tied to the school mascot.
  • Crossword design was too complex for uniforms, embroidery, and signage.

Research & Insights

Recognition

Successful school/team marks are bold, simple, and instantly tied to the mascot.

Scalability

Single-letter or monogram approaches reproduce cleanly across print methods and sizes.

Cost & Craft

Fewer fine details reduce stitch count and printing complexity, lowering cost.

Consistency

Clear usage rules (spacing, color, backgrounds) prevent drift over time.

Collaboration & Constraints

I facilitated conversations with the coach to evaluate options side-by-side: crossword vs. single-letter vs. wordmark. We reviewed pros/cons for readability, tradition, and production. With stakeholder input (coach + principal), we aligned on keeping “Wildcats” and pursuing a clean “W” system that could flex across contexts.

  • Crossword → meaningful, but hard to read, scale, or reproduce affordably.
  • “Engage” → inspiring, but disconnected from school identity and mascot.
  • Wildcats “W” → instantly recognizable, consistent with school branding, cost-effective.

This discussion allowed us to find middle ground: keeping the Wildcats identity while updating the logo for a modern, professional look.

Sketches & Exploration

I explored typography, stroke weight, and counterforms to find a “W” that felt athletic and contemporary without overly trendy details. Early roughs helped move the discussion from idea to tangible direction.

Final Logo System

The final system centers on a bold “W” primary mark with a horizontal variant for banners and warm-ups. Clean edges, balanced spacing, and high-contrast colorways ensure legibility across fabric, vinyl, and print. The system was approved by the coach and principal and rolled out on uniforms and gear.

Usage Guidelines (Summary)

  • Clear space: Maintain “W” height padding around the mark.
  • Color: Primary dark on light; approved inversions for dark apparel.
  • Don’ts: No outlines, shadows, or stretching; avoid busy backgrounds.
  • File types: Vector (SVG/PDF) for print; PNG for digital.

Impact

  • Stakeholder alignment: Coach and principal approved final system.
  • Real-world rollout: Deployed on uniforms, warm-ups, banners, and bags.
  • Consistency: A simple system + guidelines reduced ad-hoc variations.
  • Pride & identity: Clear, unified visual boosted team presence at events.

Reflection & Next Steps

This project strengthened my client collaboration and production-aware design skills. If I iterate, I’ll package a short brand kit (downloadable assets + quick-start PDF) and extend the system to social templates and event signage.

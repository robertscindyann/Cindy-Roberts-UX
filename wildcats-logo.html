Case Study: Wildcats Dance Team Rebrand

Project Overview

I was asked to design a new logo for the Wildcats Dance Team, a high school group seeking to refresh their identity. The coach wanted to explore a name change (“Engage”) and a crossword-style logo, but the team was already known as the Wildcats, the same as the school mascot. My role was to create a design that balanced her vision with school traditions and practical branding needs.

The Problem

  • Coach wanted a crossword-inspired design and considered renaming the team to “Engage.”
  • “Engage” carried meaning but risked losing recognition tied to the school mascot.
  • Crossword design was too complex for uniforms, embroidery, and signage.

Research & Inspiration

To ground my recommendations, I analyzed both college and high school dance team logos. The trend was clear: bold, simple marks (often a single letter) create instant recognition and scale well across apparel and digital platforms. This research gave me the evidence I needed to persuade stakeholders.

Pushback & Collaboration

The coach was initially attached to her crossword sketch and the idea of a new name. I presented side-by-side comparisons of her concepts and simplified single-letter designs, walking through pros and cons:

  • Crossword → meaningful, but hard to read, scale, or reproduce affordably.
  • “Engage” → inspiring, but disconnected from school identity and mascot.
  • Wildcats “W” → instantly recognizable, consistent with school branding, cost-effective.

This discussion allowed us to find middle ground: keeping the Wildcats identity while updating the logo for a modern, professional look.

Design Process: Sketches & Concepts

I began the design process by sketching multiple ideas to explore typography, mascot integration, and layout options. These explorations helped visualize different directions and refine toward a simpler, scalable identity that could adapt across uniforms, banners, and digital media.

The Solution

The final logo features a bold, single “W” design representing the Wildcats. It was approved by the coach and principal, and rolled out across uniforms, warm-ups, and banners. This gave me real-world launch experience, managing not only design but stakeholder alignment and production constraints.

Reflection

  • Learned how to navigate client pushback and align design with brand strategy.
  • Demonstrated the value of research in persuading stakeholders.
  • Considered accessibility factors like contrast and legibility at multiple scales.
  • Gained real-world launch experience — logo is now in active use on apparel and materials.
  • The team and community quickly adopted the new design, increasing school spirit and creating consistency across events.
